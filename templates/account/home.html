Nutrition Benefits

The core benefits of proper nutrition are:
Performance
- Better quality results in the long run.
- Increased Performances
Recovery
- Fewer injuries
- Can train harder
- Get increased benefits from training

Food Quantity

3 main Macronutrients (sources of calories):

Protein
- Aid recovery and build muscle tissue
- Chicken, Beef, Pork, Eggs, Natural Yogurt
- Body can only obsurb 30grams of protein per meal
---Dont think that by eating more protein you'll get better benefits.
---For protein value, 1 chicken breast is better then 2!!
- Aim for 4 fist sized portions per day.

Fat
- Small amount needed fot vitamins absorbtion and energy.
- Nuts, seeds, Olive oil, Egg yokes

Carbohydrates
- Our main energy source
- Rice, Potatos, Sweet Potatoes, Pasts, Bread, Oats, Fruit
- Two fist sized portions per day (Increase/Decrease depending on Training load)

Food Quality

- Aim for a wide range of fruits and vegeatables.
- 3 portions per day is what you should be aiming for.

Food Timing

The timing of our meals are vital to getting the best out of ourselves.
- BEFORE Training
- Aim to fuel the body but avoid the feeling of fullness during the session.
-Pre-exercise meals should contain:
---A source of carbs (To help fuel the body for the session)
---A source protein (Help avoid muscle breakdown and aid recovery after)
---Low fat & fibre to allow quicker digestion.
-AFTER Training
-Again take on carbs and protein.
-VITAL that this is consumed within 30 minutes of completeing the session.

Protein as a Supplement

Think its important to stress that supplements only account for 5% of nutritional value when it comes to recovery.
Although it's a easy way to get the required protein on board, I can't stress the importance of correct eating after exercise.
Here are some tips and advice on how to get the best from taking the supplement.

1) Why do I need protein?
- On average, it comprises about 20% of your total body weight.
- Protein makes up part of the structure of every cell and tissue in your body (including internal organs, muscle tissue, tendons, skin, hair and nails)

2) Do beginners need more protein than experiences athletes?
- Basically the answer is yes, beginners have slightly higher requirements for protein per kg body weight compared to experienced athletes
- When you begin a program your protein needs will rise due to the increase in protein turnover.
- After about 3 weeks of training, the body adapts to the exercise as the body becomes more efficient at recycling protein.

3) Protein food vs Supplements.
- Food sources of protein, such as milk are just as effective
- A study with women found that drinking skimmed milk after resistance exercise for 12 weeks reduced body fat, increased lean mass and strength

Recovery Tips

Recovery is just as important as the actual exercise carried out.
It's vital that the body is given time to rest after exercise.
The following are simple aids that can speed up recovery and help reduce the soreness we feel after exercise.

1) Get to a pool
Getting to a pool the day after a heavy session/match is brilliant for your recovery.
20/30 minutes in the pool will given nutrients to the muscles and, improve blood circulation.
2) Brisk walk
A simple 15/20 minute walk around the block post exercise will stop the muscles from stiffening up and
help to increase blood flow around the body.
3) Foam rolling
Foam rolling can help ease the pain, take out knots and essentially give you a deep tissue massage.
Think of it as ironing out a shirt with lots of creases.

Advice
- If your main goal is to build muscle, allowing the body to deal with the pain will push the body to repair better.
-If your main goal is performance (GAA/soccer/Rugby player for example) and you train on the pitch 2/3 times a week,
play a match at the weekend and have 2/3 gym sessions a week.
I would use these methods a lot as your main priority is performance, injury prevention and maintenance.

Beginners Guide

These small details have large effects on our ability to perform and achieve.

1) Sleep
- The aim is to get 6-8 sleep hours unbroken.
- An easy way of helping this is working backwards if you're up at 7.
You want to be going to sleep around 12. So 30 minutes before bed, put the phone down
(the light in our phones confuse our brains and signals the brain to stop releasing melatonin,
which is a hormone released when it's time to sleep).
- The ideal setting is a cool dark room with no light.

2) Water
- 2-3 litres a day (an easy way is taking a sip every 15 minutes or so).
- Water carries nutrients to cells and carries waste from the cells.
- Helping to maintain temperature, improve cardiac output, improve endurance and even gives a less pronounced increase in our heart rate.

3) Goals
- "The goal is to keep the goal the goal"- Dan Jon. So if your goal is to enjoy exercise, pick exercises you enjoy (classes, running, weight training).
- If you have more specific goals such as getting faster and being a better runner, don't waste time doing exercises that make you a 'better runner', get out there and run.
- If you want to get a heavier bench press, then bench more often.
